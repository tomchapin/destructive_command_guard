## Summary of Findings

*   **Total Patterns**: ~380
*   **Backtracking Required**: ~58 patterns (~15%)
*   **Linear-Safe**: ~322 patterns (~85%)

### Primary Drivers for Backtracking

1.  **Path Traversal Prevention**: Used heavily in `core.filesystem` and `safe.cleanup` to prevent attacks like `rm -rf /tmp/../etc`.
    *   Pattern: `(?![^\s]*\.\.)`
2.  **Flag Exclusion**: Used in many packs to distinguish destructive commands from their dry-run counterparts or safer variants.
    *   Pattern: `(?!.*--dry-run)`, `(?!.*--staged)`
3.  **Command Disambiguation**: Used in `core.git` to distinguish `git checkout` (destructive) from `git checkout -b` (safe).
    *   Pattern: `(?!-b\b)`

### Recommendation

The majority of patterns (85%) are linear-safe and can benefit from the optimization in `git_safety_guard-ksk.2` (adopting `CompiledRegex`). The remaining 15% correctly identified by `needs_backtracking_engine` will continue to use `fancy-regex` transparently.
